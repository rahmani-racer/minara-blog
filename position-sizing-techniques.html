Position Sizing Techniques

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026

Intro

Proper sizing converts a signal into a controlled risk. Learn practical sizing methods that traders use.

Step-by-step

  1. Choose a risk percentage per trade (e.g., 0.5–2%).
  2. Calculate position size from stop distance and risk amount.
  3. Adjust for correlation and total portfolio exposure.

GOLD example

With $10,000 account and 1% risk, and a 50 USD stop on XAU/USD, size = (100 USD) / 50 USD = 2 units (example).

Common mistakes

  • Using fixed lot sizes without recalculating risk.

Pro tips

  • Automate sizing in your entry checklist to avoid manual errors.
