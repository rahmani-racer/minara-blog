Leverage and Margin Explained

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026 — Minara Blog

Problem-based introduction

Leverage dekh kar beginners excited ho jate hain — "10x, 50x" — lekin margin ka galat istemal account blow-up tak le ja sakta hai. Is article mein simple Hinglish me leverage, margin aur practical GOLD (XAU/USD) examples se samjhayenge ki safe use kaise karein.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Leverage: Ratio jo broker deti hai (e.g., 1:100) — aap chhote capital se bada exposure le sakte ho.
  2. Margin: Required deposit to open a leveraged position (exposure / leverage).
  3. Maintenance margin & margin call: Broker minimum equity level below which position may be closed.

Real trading logic (GOLD example)

Example: Account $1000, leverage 1:100, you can open $100,000 notional. If XAU/USD moves $10 against you and your contract size / pip value leads to $50 per lot per $1 move (broker-specific), losses magnify quickly. Always calculate absolute dollar risk before using leverage.

Common Mistakes

  • Using maximum available leverage on every trade.
  • Confusing leverage with account size—risk per trade should be absolute dollar amount.

Pro Tips

  • Use low leverage and small position sizes when learning.
  • Calculate worst-case scenario loss and ensure it fits your risk tolerance.

Risk Warning

Leverage increases both potential profit and potential loss. Use stop-loss and risk management.

SEO FAQs

1. Leverage kya hai?
Leverage allows larger market exposure relative to account capital.
2. Margin kaise calculate karein?
Margin = exposure / leverage (check broker contract specs).
3. Kya high leverage kabhi safe hota hai?
Only for experienced traders with strict risk controls; beginners should avoid high leverage.
