Trading Costs Explained

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026 — Minara Blog

Problem-based introduction

Costs eat returns. Traders often underestimate spread, commission, swap and slippage. This article explains each cost component clearly and shows how to estimate them for GOLD trades.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Spread: Bid-ask difference—immediate cost on entry and exit.
  2. Commission: Per trade fee, common with ECN brokers.
  3. Swap/overnight: Cost or credit for holding positions past rollover.
  4. Slippage: Fill difference from expected price.

Real trading logic (GOLD example)

Estimate total cost: spread + expected slippage + commission + swap (if holding). For swing trades, swap can be meaningful; for intraday, spread + commission dominate.

Image-based examples (mandatory)

Common Mistakes

  • Not including overnight swap in multi-day trades.
  • Comparing advertised spreads without real execution data.

Pro Tips

  • Measure real spreads on demo during your trading hours and include an average slippage estimate.
  • Factor costs into expectancy calculations—small edge may be nullified by high costs.

Risk Warning

Costs vary by broker and market conditions; always verify and include them in trade planning.

SEO FAQs

1. Trading costs kaise calculate karein?
Sum of spread, commission, slippage, and swap (if holding overnight).
2. Swap kaise kam karein?
Close trades before rollover or use swap-free accounts if available and suitable.
3. Kya broker change se cost kam ho jayega?
Possibly—compare execution and real spread+slippage data, not just marketing numbers.
