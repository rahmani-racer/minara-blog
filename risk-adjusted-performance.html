Risk-adjusted Performance

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026

Intro

Absolute returns aren't enough—measure returns relative to risk for a clearer comparison between strategies.

Step-by-step

  1. Calculate Sharpe ratio to measure excess returns per unit volatility.
  2. Use Sortino to focus on downside risk.
  3. Compare strategies using risk-adjusted metrics, not raw returns.

GOLD example

Compare two XAU/USD strategies: one with higher raw returns but higher volatility vs a smoother, lower-volatility strategy; risk-adjusted metrics may prefer the latter.
