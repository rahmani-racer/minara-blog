How Forex Brokers Work

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026 — Minara Blog

Problem-based introduction

Choosing a broker and understanding its model matters for execution and costs. Is your fill coming from ECN, or is it an internalised market maker? Is the spread fixed or variable? We'll explain practically with GOLD (XAU/USD) examples.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Broker models: Market maker vs ECN/STP—difference in routing and fees.
  2. Order execution: How orders are matched and where liquidity comes from.
  3. Additional services: Margins, swaps, reporting, and client support.

Real trading logic (GOLD example)

Test brokers on demo and compare fills for XAU/USD around typical trade times; measure spread, slippage, and execution speed to choose the right partner for your strategy.

Image-based examples (mandatory)

Common Mistakes

  • Choosing broker solely on marketing without testing execution.
  • Ignoring hidden fees like inactivity or withdrawal charges.

Pro Tips

  • Collect execution reports and compare during your target trading hours.
  • Prefer regulated brokers and clear T&Cs for client protection.

Risk Warning

Broker selection affects execution and risk; choose carefully and test on demo before committing funds.

SEO FAQs

1. ECN kya hota hai?
ECN is an electronic communication network that routes orders to liquidity providers with often tighter raw spreads plus commission.
2. Market maker kya karta hai?
Market makers provide price and may internalise orders; they can offer fixed spreads but watch for widening during news.
3. Broker regulation kaise check karein?
Check regulator license on broker website and verify on regulator portal (FCA, ASIC, CySEC, etc.).
