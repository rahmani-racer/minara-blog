Currency Pairs Explained

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026 — Minara Blog

Problem-based introduction

Beginners often ask: "Currency pair kya hota hai?" Basic samajh bina aap trades galat size kar sakte hain ya price meaning galat samajh sakte hain. Is article mein simple Hinglish mein base/quote concept, major/minor/cross pairs aur GOLD (XAU/USD) ka context samjhayenge.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Base and Quote: In EUR/USD, EUR is base, USD is quote. Price 1.1200 means 1 EUR = 1.1200 USD.
  2. Major pairs: Most traded pairs like EUR/USD, USD/JPY — tight spreads and high liquidity.
  3. Minor and cross pairs: Pairs without USD as one leg (e.g., EUR/GBP) — watch liquidity and wider spreads.
  4. Commodity pairs & GOLD: GOLD is quoted as XAU/USD — treat it similarly to a major pair with its own volatility and session behaviour.

Real trading logic (GOLD example)

Example: XAU/USD at 1950 — if USD weakens, XAU/USD often rises. For a trader, understanding which currency drives the pair helps make better directional calls.

  1. Compare XAU/USD with USD index — inverse correlation sometimes appears.
  2. Check session liquidity before trading cross pairs; GOLD liquidity may be better around London/NY sessions.

Common Mistakes

  • Misreading base/quote and miscalculating position value.
  • Trading illiquid crosses at large sizes without checking spreads.

Pro Tips

  • Use majors for tighter spreads when starting out.
  • Watch correlated instruments — GOLD and USD pairs often interact.

Risk Warning

Understand pair mechanics before trading live; incorrect assumptions cost money.

SEO FAQs

1. Currency pair kya hota hai?
Base and quote currencies ka combination jisme price quoted hota hai.
2. Gold is a currency pair?
XAU/USD is commodity quoted in USD; treat like a tradable pair.
3. Which pairs beginners should trade?
Major pairs for tighter spreads; practice on demo first.
