Risk Management Principles

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026

Problem intro

Risk management decides survival. Without rules, even a good strategy will fail during drawdowns.

Step-by-step

  1. Define max risk per trade (percent of capital).
  2. Set stops based on structure and volatility.
  3. Limit correlated exposure across positions.

GOLD example

For XAU/USD, use ATR-based stops and limit position size so that risk per trade is <=1% of capital.

Common mistakes

  • Using arbitrary stop sizes not tied to market structure.

Pro tips

  • Keep a daily risk summary and track max adverse excursion for each trade.

Risk warning

Risk rules reduce ruin probability but don't guarantee profits.
