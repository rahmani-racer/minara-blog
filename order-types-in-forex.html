Order Types in Forex

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026 — Minara Blog

Problem-based introduction

Order type nahi samjhe toh galat price pe entry ya exit ho sakta hai. Yeh article simple Hinglish mein market, limit, stop, stop-limit aur advanced orders explain karega with GOLD examples.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Market order: Immediate execution at available price.
  2. Limit order: Execute only at specified better price.
  3. Stop order: Trigger market order when price reaches stop level (used for stop-loss).
  4. Stop-limit: Trigger limit order on stop — avoids slippage but may not fill.

Real trading logic (GOLD example)

Example: Use a limit buy near support for XAU/USD to get better fills; use stop-loss as market order if you prefer guaranteed exit, or stop-limit if you want to avoid bad fills during thin markets.

Image-based examples (mandatory)

Common Mistakes

  • Using stop-limit during highly volatile news where fills are needed.
  • Placing market orders with large size during low liquidity leading to slippage.

Pro Tips

  • Use limit entries for planned entries; use market stop-loss for guaranteed exit in emergencies.
  • Test order behaviour on your broker's demo to see how stop and limit orders are executed.

Risk Warning

Order types behave differently across brokers; test them to avoid unexpected fills and slippage.

SEO FAQs

1. Market order kya hota hai?
Immediate execution at current available price.
2. Stop-limit vs stop-market ka difference?
Stop-limit triggers a limit order; stop-market triggers a market order at stop.
3. Kaunsa order newbies ke liye safe hai?
Use a mix—limit for entries, market stop for emergency exits depending on execution needs.
