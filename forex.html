Forex Knowledge

Forex market basics se advance concepts tak — simple Hindi/Hinglish me, beginners ke liye clear explanation ke saath.

Forex Kya Hai? (What is Forex?)

Forex (Foreign Exchange Market) duniya ka sabse bada financial market hai, daily turnover $7+ trillion. Yahan currencies trade hoti hain — jab aap ek currency buy karte ho, automatically dusri sell hoti hai (currency pairs: EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/INR).

24/5 open: Asia session (Tokyo open 5:30 AM IST), Europe (London 1:30 PM IST), America (NY 6:30 PM IST). Weekend close.

Currency Pairs Samajhein

  • Major pairs: EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY — highest liquidity, tight spreads (0.1–0.8 pips ECN).
  • Minor pairs (cross): EUR/GBP, AUD/NZD — no USD; wider spreads, moderate volume.
  • Exotic pairs: USD/INR, USD/ZAR, EUR/TRY — illiquid, spreads 5–50 pips; fundamentals-driven.

Quote structure: EUR/USD 1.0850 → 1 Euro = 1.0850 USD. Base currency (left) vs quote currency (right). Bid (sell price) vs Ask (buy price); difference = spread.

Pips aur Lot Size

  • Pip: Price movement ki smallest unit. Most pairs: 4th decimal (0.0001). JPY pairs: 2nd decimal (0.01). Pipette = 0.1 pip (5th decimal).
  • Lot sizes:
    • Standard lot: 100,000 units (1 pip = $10 for USD pairs).
    • Mini lot: 10,000 units (1 pip = $1).
    • Micro lot: 1,000 units (1 pip = $0.10).
    • Nano lot: 100 units (1 pip = $0.01).

Example: EUR/USD 1.0850 → 1.0860 (10-pip move). Trading 0.1 lot (mini) = $1/pip × 10 = $10 profit (long trade).

Leverage aur Margin

Leverage: Broker se borrowed capital. 1:100 leverage → $100 = $10,000 trade control. Amplifies profit aur loss.

Margin: Collateral locked for trade. $10,000 trade @ 1:100 leverage = $100 margin required. Margin call agar balance margin requirement se niche jaaye.

Risk: Leverage high → losses bhi high. Beginners 1:10 to 1:50 recommended; avoid 1:500+.

Technical vs Fundamental Analysis

Technical Analysis: Charts, indicators, price action se future predict karo.

  • Support/Resistance: levels jahan price reverse hota hai.
  • Indicators: RSI (overbought/oversold), MACD (momentum), Moving Averages (trend direction).
  • Candlestick patterns: Doji, Engulfing, Pin Bar — reversals signal.

Fundamental Analysis: Economic news, central bank policy, geopolitical events.

  • NFP (Non-Farm Payrolls): US jobs data; USD volatility 50–100 pips.
  • Interest rates: Fed/ECB/BoE rate hike → currency strong; rate cut → weak.
  • GDP, CPI (inflation): Strong economy → currency appreciation.

Risk Management Essentials

  • Stop-loss mandatory: Har trade me fixed SL. Never hold "hope" ki price wapas aayegi.
  • Risk per trade: 1–2% of account. $1000 account → max $10-$20 risk/trade. Scalpers 0.5%.
  • Risk:Reward ratio: Minimum 1:1.5; ideal 1:2 ya 1:3. 10-pip risk → 20-30 pip target.
  • Position sizing formula: Lot size = (Account × Risk%) / (Stop distance in pips × pip value).

Example: $1000 account, 2% risk ($20), EUR/USD 20-pip SL, pip value $1/mini lot → Lot = 20 / (20×1) = 1 mini lot (0.1 standard).

Forex Broker Selection

  1. Regulation: FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), CySEC (Cyprus). India: no direct SEBI forex retail; offshore brokers common but risky. Check regulation status.
  2. Spreads aur commissions: ECN/STP accounts: raw spreads 0.1–0.5 + $3-$7 commission/lot. Market maker: wider spreads (1–3 pips), no commission.
  3. Execution speed: Slippage < 0.5 pip during news. Test demo 50 trades; measure avg slippage.
  4. Withdrawal speed: 24-48 hrs ideal. Avoid brokers with withdrawal complaints.
  5. Platform: MetaTrader 4/5 (most common), cTrader (advanced). Check mobile app quality.

Common Beginner Mistakes

  • Over-leveraging: 1:500 leverage + 50% account risk = wipeout in 2-3 trades. Limit 1:50 max.
  • No trading plan: Random entries based on "feeling". Define setup, entry, SL, TP before trade.
  • Revenge trading: Loss ke baad bada lot size leke "recover" karna. Leads to bigger loss.
  • Ignoring fundamentals: Scalping during NFP/FOMC; spreads explode, slippage 10+ pips.
  • Chasing price: FOMO entry after 50-pip move; often reversal point. Wait for pullback.

Forex Trading Roadmap (90-Day Beginner Plan)

  1. Week 1-2: Learn basics — currency pairs, pips, lot size, leverage, margin. Read articles, watch YouTube tutorials. Open demo account.
  2. Week 3-4: Study one strategy deeply — price action (support/resistance + candlesticks) OR indicator-based (RSI + MACD). Practice 50 demo trades.
  3. Week 5-8: Backtest strategy on TradingView/MT4; 100 trades historical data. Measure winrate, avg R:R. Refine entry/exit rules.
  4. Week 9-12: Forward test demo 200 trades; journal every trade (setup, emotion, result). Target 50%+ winrate with 1:2 R:R.
  5. Month 4+: Micro live account ($100-$200); trade nano/micro lots. Scale up only after 3 months consistent profit.

India-Specific Forex Context

Legal status: SEBI allows forex derivatives (USD/INR futures) on NSE/BSE; lot size large (~$1000), margin high. Retail spot forex trading offshore (international brokers) legally grey area; RBI discourages capital outflow.

Taxation: Forex gains taxable as speculative income (if intraday) or capital gains (if positions held). Offshore broker profits must be declared in ITR; consult CA for exact treatment.

Alternatives: Trade USD/INR futures on NSE for legal compliance; lower leverage (1:10-1:20), but regulated. Many Indians trade offshore for higher leverage/smaller lot sizes — understand legal risks.

Pro Tips for Indian Traders

  • Session timing: Best for Indian traders: London open (1:30 PM) to NY close (1 AM IST). Avoid Asia-only (low volatility).
  • INR pairs: USD/INR moves 10-30 pips/day; low volatility vs majors (50-100 pips). Better for swing; not scalping.
  • News calendar: Track US NFP (1st Friday 6 PM IST), FOMC (rate decision 11:30 PM IST), RBI policy (10:30 AM IST for USD/INR impact).
  • Broker deposits: Use Skrill/Neteller/crypto for offshore brokers; Indian banks may block direct forex broker transactions.

FAQ — Forex Beginners

1. Forex trading India mein legal hai?
NSE/BSE pe USD/INR futures legal (SEBI regulated). Offshore spot forex brokers legally grey area; RBI discourages but no explicit ban on individuals. Trade at own risk; declare profits in ITR.
2. Minimum kitne paise se start kar sakte hain?
Demo account free; live $10-$50 se shuru (micro/nano lots). Realistically $200-$500 recommended for proper risk management (2% risk = $4-$10/trade).
3. Kitna profit realistic hai monthly?
Beginners: 2-5% monthly consistent realistic. 10%+ possible but high risk. Avoid promises like "50% monthly"—scams. Pro traders target 3-8% monthly with low drawdown.
4. Technical ya Fundamental analysis better hai?
Combine both. Technicals for entry/exit timing; fundamentals for trend direction. Example: Fed hawkish (fundamental) → USD bullish; wait for support bounce (technical) to enter long USD pairs.
5. Scalping vs Swing trading — beginners ke liye kaunsa?
Swing trading easier for beginners: fewer trades, less screen time, wider SL (20-50 pips vs 5-10 scalp). Scalping demands tight spreads, fast execution, intense focus; advanced level.
6. Demo account se live mein switch kab karein?
After 3-6 months demo with 200+ trades, 50%+ winrate, consistent R:R 1:2+, journaling habit strong. Start live with micro lots; scale slowly.
7. Kaunse pairs beginners ke liye best?
EUR/USD, GBP/USD (highest liquidity, tight spreads, moderate volatility). Avoid exotic pairs initially (wide spreads, unpredictable moves).
8. Stop-loss hunting real hai ya myth?
Yes, liquidity hunting hota hai at obvious levels (round numbers, visible swing highs/lows). Solution: place SL 5-10 pips beyond obvious zone; use limit orders instead of tight market SL.
