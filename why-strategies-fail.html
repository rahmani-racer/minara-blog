Why Strategies Fail

Last Updated: 16 Jan 2026 — Minara Blog

Problem-based introduction

Many traders build strategies that look good historically but fail live. This article explores common reasons (overfitting, ignoring costs, psychological mismatch) and practical fixes, illustrated with GOLD examples.

Step-by-step explanation

  1. Overfitting: Optimising parameters to past noise not future edge.
  2. Ignoring costs: Spread, commission and slippage can destroy edge if not accounted for.
  3. Psychology mismatch: Strategy rules must match trader's capacity to follow them under stress.

Real trading logic (GOLD example)

Backtest a GOLD breakout strategy including realistic spreads, slippage, and execution delays. If live performance differs, examine parameter robustness and reduce over-optimization.

Image-based examples (mandatory)

Annotated backtest vs live P&L comparison and checklist for fixes.

Common Mistakes

  • Cherry-picking best parameters without out-of-sample testing.
  • Not including realistic transaction costs in simulation.

Pro Tips

  • Use out-of-sample testing, walk-forward analysis, and include costs.
  • Start small live and compare metrics; iterate only when you have consistent evidence.

Risk Warning

No strategy is guaranteed—evaluate robustness and preserve capital during testing phases.

SEO FAQs

1. Strategy overfitting kaise check karein?
Use out-of-sample data, cross-validation, and avoid excessive parameter tuning.
2. Kya Monte Carlo useful hai?
Yes—use it to test distribution of outcomes under variable trade sequences.
